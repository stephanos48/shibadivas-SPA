Shiba Inu Characteristics


General Temperament / Appearance


The Shiba is the smallest of the Japanese native breeds of dog and was originally developed for hunting by sight and scent in the dense undergrowth of Japan’s mountainous areas. Alert and agile with keen senses, he is also an excellent watchdog and companion. His frame is compact with well-developed muscles. Males and females are distinctly different in appearance: males are masculine without coarseness, females are feminine without weakness of structure.


Physical Characteristics / Lifespan


Height

Male: 14–17 inches (35–43 cm), Female: 13–16 inches (33–41 cm)

Weight

Male: 18–24 lbs (8–11 kg), Female: 15–20 lbs (6.8–9 kg)

Lifespan

12 – 15 years

Colors

Black & Tan, Red Sesame, Cream, Black Sesame, Red, Sesame


Fur / Coat


Double coated with the outer coat being stiff and straight and the undercoat soft and thick. Fur is short and even on face, ears, and legs. Guard hairs stand off the body are about 1½ to 2 inches in length at the withers. Tail hair is slightly longer and stands open in a brush. It is preferred that the Shiba be presented in a natural state. Unfortunately, Shibas are not hypoallergenic. Shibas shed lightly twice a year: once in the spring and once in the fall.
